BuildGreen

Data-Driven Carbon Metrics for Infrastructure Architecture

Did you know? Global concrete application contributes to roughly 8% of total worldwide CO₂ emissions. In past historical generations, the reliance on heavy cement matrices was drastically minor. Today, structural concrete stands as the second most consumed resource on Earth after water. Academic models warn that exploding urban populations will multiply this volume exponentially by 2050.

The BuildGreen algorithm was custom engineered by Sugdiena Bozorboeva to empower pioneering designers to mitigate construction air footprint through advanced material optimization.

Methodological System Architecture

The calculations engineered into BuildGreen parse empirical carbon data coefficients compiled across the global Inventory of Carbon and Energy (ICE) Ledger and standard structural directives published by the American Concrete Institute (ACI). Transport freight variables model a standard heavy-vehicle logistics coefficient calibrated at 0.12 kg of CO₂ per metric-ton kilometer. Ecosystem sequestration data sets adopt the US Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) parameters modeling localized organic absorption rates.
